Project Overview

HADES is an embedded monitoring system designed to detect situations where a heat source remains active while no one is present to supervise it.

Our approach is to monitor temperature and human presence together so the device can recognize a potentially dangerous unattended condition before it becomes a fire emergency.

Unlike systems that only react after smoke or fire is already present, HADES is intended to warn the user earlier through a local alarm and IoT-based notification.

Why HADES?

Neglected stoves cause over 70,000 house fires in the United States each year, which makes unattended cooking a major household safety concern.

Traditional devices such as smoke detectors and sprinklers are important, but they typically respond only after a fire has already started.

HADES is meant to act earlier by identifying risky conditions before the situation escalates.

Objectives and Existing Products

Sensors Used

Temperature

MLX90614

HADES uses the MLX90614, a contactless infrared temperature sensor, to detect heat from a distance.

Presence

LD2410

Human presence is detected using the LD2410, a 24 GHz millimeter wave radar sensor.

Demo Configuration

For demonstration, the temperature threshold was reduced to 200°F and the warning period was reduced to 5 seconds.

The planned final testing configuration is 100°C with a 60-second warning timer.

The radar is currently very sensitive, so unattended testing is planned in a safe and controlled environment.
